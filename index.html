Part 1

Mental Models, Not Magic

The real starting point

This talk is not about tools

It's about mental models

How you think about what a tool is, what it can do, and how you work with it over time.

The Google Analogy

  • Google made the internet's corpus usable
  • Getting "good at Google" wasn't about features
  • It was about internalizing a mental model

Learning to Think Like Google

What you first tried:

"Please give me directions to the nearest ice cream store which is called Molly Moon."

What you learned:

Molly Moon directions Google Maps

Chatbots: The Next Step

ChatGPT and Claude are closer to:

  • A savant friend
  • Who knows the entire corpus
  • And can reason across domains

The Real Leap: Execution

With coding tools, agents, and workflows...

You are no longer talking to a friend.

You are directing digital employees.

At Scale

  • You don't do the execution
  • You don't even do most of the coordination
  • You become a top-level director

The Expanded Mental Model

I now have access to the world's corpus of knowledge and the ability to make things digitally.

Key Insight

Just like Google, this understanding does not arrive by watching.

It only arrives by using the tools repeatedly until the mental model clicks.

Unlike Google, the cost of not developing this literacy is much higher.

Part 4

The Map Itself

Depth, Not Identity

Important Clarification

This is NOT:

  • Technical vs non-technical people
  • Smart vs not smart

This IS:

  • Different depths of engagement
  • Different willingness to go further

The Real Divider

None of this is inherently hard.

The real divider is willingness to ask questions about things you don't yet understand.

Non-Technical Track

Depth Without Coding

Levels 0–8

Level 0

Avoidance

Historical echoes of early internet skepticism.

Level 1

AI as Enhanced Google

  • Q&A
  • Summaries
  • Explanations
  • Brainstorming

The goal here is confidence, not mastery.

Level 2

AI as a Thought Partner

  • Thinking out loud
  • Writing assistance
  • Reflection and synthesis

"Oh — this is different."

Level 3

Context Engineering

  • How much context helps vs hurts
  • Structuring inputs for better performance
  • Being explicit about desired outputs
  • Using Projects (Claude / ChatGPT)
  • Choosing the right model

Results start to feel reliable.

Level 4

Capture & Learning

  • Voice dictation (Wispr Flow)
  • Automatic meeting notes (Granola)
  • AI as an active tutor
  • Voice mode for learning
Separate thinking from formatting. Cleanup comes later.
Level 5

AI-Enabled Browsing

  • AI layers attached to the browser
  • Research without breaking flow
  • Context maintained across tabs

Autonomy begins before APIs or code.

Level 6

Media & Creative Production

  • Image generation (Midjourney, Nano Banana)
  • Iterative refinement
  • Model-specific tuning

The skill here is not prompting — it's directing taste.

Level 7

No-Code Automations

  • Workflow tools (n8n, Gum Loop)
  • Automating personal & work processes
  • Surprisingly deep without writing code
Level 8

Natural-Language Software

  • Tools like Lovable, Bolt, AI Studio
  • Creating front-end systems
  • Without understanding internals
